The Yunaka Foundation is a powerful and massive corporation based within [[Mondral]] although they are international influence as , operating across numerous industries including weapon manufacturing & research, magical research, arcane infrastructure, and private security ([[Yunaka Security Force]]).

However, the Foundation has also expanded into many ordinary commercial industries, including food production, transportation such as [[Arcane Train]] and [[Arcane Airships]], construction, medical services, entertainment, communication networks, banking, education, pharmaceuticals, household goods, energy distribution, and public/private infrastructure.
### History
##### Creation — Mondralian Fight for Freedom
The Yunaka Foundation was established by the [[Yunaka Family]], a powerful family that rose to prominence during the [[Mondralian Fight for Freedom]], at 1381 to 1890 [[After Calamity (AC)]]. During the conflict, the family supplied weapons and magical equipment to the Mondralian fighters, greatly contributing to the war effort and rapidly expanding their influence.

Following the creation of [[Mondral]], the Yunaka Foundation helped develop much of the nation’s public and private industries. Many [[Daimyo]], [[Shougen]], and even [[Samurai]] relied upon the Yunaka Family for their expertise in gunsmithing and magical item creation, which became the Foundation’s primary specialty.

As their influence grew, the Foundation expanded further through the creation of the [[Yunaka Research Department]], allowing them to advance both magical and industrial technologies at an unprecedented rate.
##### Expansion & Sakichi Era
The Yunaka Foundation expanded rapidly as government-funded projects increasingly relied upon the Foundation’s industrial capabilities, magical expertise, and production speed.

However, the Yunaka Family itself faced an internal issue. Many members of the family were deeply devoted to their crafts and artistic pursuits, causing concerns over succession, as few were interested in producing heirs. Eventually, [[Soichiro Yunaka]] and his wife gave birth to a successor, [[Sakichi Yunaka]], in 1450 [[After Calamity (AC)]].

Under [[Sakichi Yunaka]], the Foundation transformed into the massive corporation it is known as today. While previous generations mainly focused on weapon manufacturing and magical research, Sakichi recognized the importance of controlling broader industries. Under his leadership, the Foundation aggressively expanded through acquisitions, partnerships, and the establishment of new branches across numerous fields.

This rapid expansion alarmed many politicians and powerful figures. However, [[Sakichi Yunaka]] circumvented much of this opposition by establishing the [[Yunaka Board of Director]] and opening portions of the Foundation to outside investment with support from the [[Merchant Guilds]].

As a result, many influential individuals became financially tied to the Foundation itself, making direct opposition increasingly difficult.

Sakichi also recruited talented individuals from outside the Yunaka Family, granting them wealth, influence, creative freedom, and high-ranking positions within the corporation. Having recognized that the desire to perfect one’s craft existed beyond his own bloodline, Sakichi sought to turn that ambition into both innovation and profit.
#### The Great War
[[Sakichi Yunaka]] died shortly before the beginning of [[The Great Vallarian War]] in 1501 [[After Calamity (AC)]], leaving behind a single heir, [[Konosuke Yunaka]] at the age of 35 years old at the year 1490 AC . Like many members of the [[Yunaka Family]], Sakichi devoted far more of his life to the growth of the Foundation than to his family.

Despite this, [[Konosuke Yunaka]] successfully continued his father’s legacy. Even before the outbreak of the war, he recognized the growing political instability across [[Continent of Vallarian|Valla]] and rapidly expanded the Foundation’s weapon manufacturing capabilities in preparation for a large-scale conflict.

When [[The Great Vallarian War]] began, the Yunaka Foundation became one of the primary suppliers of firearms, magical equipment, transportation, and industrial resources for [[Mondral]]. The efficiency and production capacity of the Foundation played a major role in supporting the Mondralian war effort.

The war also allowed [[Konosuke Yunaka]] to aggressively expand the Foundation’s economic influence. As the Mondralian government focused heavily on military victory, many regulations and restrictions surrounding corporate expansion were overlooked in favor of efficiency and production speed.

Using wartime profits and government reliance on Yunaka infrastructure, Konosuke acquired competing companies across numerous industries both within [[Mondral]] and throughout [[Continent of Vallarian|Valla]]. In many cases, even enemy nations indirectly relied upon Yunaka-produced goods, transportation systems, or industrial materials through third-party trade networks.

Under Konosuke’s leadership, the Foundation entered a new age of international business. The corporation expanded beyond military production into industries tied to everyday life, including food production, housing, banking, loans, medical supplies, potions, logistics, and public infrastructure.

The development of the [[Arcane Train]] and [[Arcane Airships]] further cemented Yunaka dominance, fundamentally changing transportation, trade, and warfare across the continent.

By the later years of the war, large sections of the economy had become dependent upon Yunaka industries, creating a corporate network so deeply integrated into society that dismantling the Foundation would risk destabilizing entire regions.

##### Post War Era
Following the war, [[Konosuke Yunaka]] continued expanding the Foundation’s international influence. Although the Yunaka Foundation remained primarily based within [[Mondral]], Konosuke further pursued his father’s philosophy of diversification.

Numerous new branches and subsidiaries were established, including those involved in [[Yunaka Transportation Industry]], printing, logistics, magical communication, manufacturing, and commercial services such as the Yunaka Scroll Industry and Yunaka Printing.

However, the post-war era also saw the rise of civil wars, rebellions, [[Criminal Organization]]s, and radical [[Cult]] activity throughout many regions of [[Valla]]. Rumors began circulating that portions of the Foundation were secretly profiting from these conflicts through arms dealing, transportation contracts, resource manipulation, and indirect support networks.

Some reports even suggested that certain members of the [[Yunaka Board of Director]] maintained connections with criminal groups and extremist organizations in order to increase corporate profits and expand Yunaka influence within unstable regions.

Whether the [[Yunaka Family]] itself was fully aware of these activities remains uncertain. Some believe these operations were conducted by rogue executives and directors acting independently under the pressure of endless corporate expansion.

Like the generations before him, [[Konosuke Yunaka]] prioritized the growth of the Foundation over his personal life. Despite this, he fathered three heirs before his death between the ages of 125 to 129:
- [[Akane Yunaka]] (Oldest)
- [[Akari Yunaka]] (Middle)
- [[Hanako Yunaka]] (Youngest)

However, [[Akane Yunaka]] died unexpectedly in 1610 [[After Calamity (AC)]], reportedly due to sudden medical complications, though rumors surrounding the incident persist to this day.

Following Akane’s death, [[Tanaka Yunaka]] inherited leadership of the Foundation at the age of 22 in 1617 [[After Calamity (AC)]], following the death of her father at 151 years old due to illness caused by overwork and old age, nearing the natural lifespan of a [[Half Elf]]. Under Tanaka’s leadership, the Foundation continued its expansion and further diversified its control across numerous industries.
