**The Black Legion** is an international terrorist organization that exists within the [[Continent of Vallarian|Land of Valla]]. It seeks to bring about the destruction of the established system. The organization emerged after the [[Great Southern Empire Collapse]], which followed the fall of the [[Great Southern Empire]].

Rather than pursuing a slow diplomatic approach, the Black Legion believes that established systems, whether in governance or among powerful factions, will not change without decisive and forceful action. As a result, its members have united under a shared conviction that only radical measures can reshape the world.

In its early stages, the Black Legion was neither powerful nor influential. However, this changed as public awareness spread across Alvavia, eventually culminating in the [[Alvavian Revolution of 1623 AC]]. The effects of the [[Mondralian Isolationism After Great War]], combined with the growing influence of the [[Yunaka Foundation]] and its expansion within [[Mondral]], further destabilized the region.

The [[Third Great Southern Shock]] then shattered the remnants of the [[Great Southern Empire]] and turned the land into a massive battleground. These events, along with numerous other crises during the early 1600s of [[After Calamity (AC)]], led to a surge in support for the Black Legion.

Additionally, their connection to the [[Ouroboros Organization]] has earned them many enemies, though many of those enemies were already unpopular among the public. As a result, various groups and individuals have aligned themselves with [[The Black Legion]], either due to shared ideals or in pursuit of a common enemy.
#### **Relationship :**
The Black Legion maintains few formal alliances or close relationships with other groups, aside from its connection to the [[Ouroboros Organization]]. This relationship is believed to go beyond simple cooperation between terrorist organizations, as their movements and actions are often closely aligned.

Despite their goal of dismantling the established system, the Black Legion still maintains indirect ties to it. Some view this as hypocritical, relying on the very structures they seek to destroy, while others argue that such connections are necessary to secure resources and maintain operational effectiveness.

The motivations of groups that associate with the Black Legion vary. In many cases, these groups see the Legion as a means to achieve their own goals if its plans succeed. Not all members within these allied groups are fully committed. Often, only smaller factions or rogue elements choose to cooperate.
##### Hostile Relation :
There are many groups that are direct enemies of the Black Legion due to its inhumane actions in pursuit of its so-called ideals. These actions are widely seen as dangerous and must be stopped by most justice authorities across the [[Continent of Vallarian]], as well as by many major non-illegal [[Factions]] within the region.

Even so, their primary enemy is the [[OPAL Group]], a division of the [[Adventurer's Guild]]. While the Guild itself remains unaligned with the Legion, the secretive OPAL Group is fully dedicated to safeguarding humanity from acts of terror. They strive for a better future through more diplomatic and controlled methods, standing in clear contrast to both the Black Legion and the [[Ouroboros Organization]].
#### **Goal & Ideals :**
The Black Legion seeks to establish a new system to replace the current one (Or some might called : anti-establishment), which they view as fundamentally flawed. Rather than pursuing reform through diplomacy, they believe that only decisive and often violent action can bring about meaningful change.

They see those in power as unwilling to relinquish control without resistance or personal gain. As a result, the Black Legion has largely abandoned peaceful methods and instead chooses to carve its own path forward through force.

To achieve their objectives, they are willing to employ any necessary strategy. This can include forming temporary alliances, even with those they ultimately intend to overthrow, or carrying out reckless operations that endanger countless lives. Such actions have become a defining trait of the organization, particularly in incidents involving the [[Ouroboros Organization]] and other factions, such as the events of the [[1623 AC Orange Corral Incidents]].
