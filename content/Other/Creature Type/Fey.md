**The Fey** are a classification of supernatural beings that originate from the [[Feywild]], a realm that exists parallel to the material world and is rarely accessed by mortal beings. Unlike mortal creatures, Fey are deeply tied to emotion, nature, and the abstract forces of imagination and story, which connect them closely to the power of [[Imaginatio]].

Their existence is often unpredictable, as they do not strictly follow the same logic or rules that govern other beings. Their affinity with [[Imaginatio]] allows them to bend the rules of reality to a significant degree, granting them access to unique and often incomprehensible forms of magic.

The Fey are not inherently good or evil. Instead, they operate on their own sense of logic, often shaped by emotion, symbolism, and personal interpretation. Their devotion to their ideals can resemble the intensity seen in [[Divine Magical Energy]], allowing some powerful Fey to manifest entirely new forms of power. As there are many [[Types of Fey]] that exist in the realm.

What may seem kind or cruel by mortal standards may hold an entirely different meaning to a Fey. As a result, interactions with them can be both wondrous and dangerous, as they are fully driven by their own ideals.

Fey are known to influence the world in subtle and indirect ways. Rather than direct conquest, they prefer manipulation, bargains, and long-term schemes, as such methods align more closely with their whimsical nature which could lead some mortal to a [[Fey-Touched Individuals]]. Their presence is often tied to places where reality is thin or heavily influenced by strong natural or emotional energy.
#### **Weakness & Disruption :**
Despite their power, Fey are not without weaknesses. Their reliance on emotion, symbolism, and perception can be used against them.

- Cold iron is often disruptive to their form and magic
- Logical or structured environments may weaken their influence
- Breaking symbolic meaning can destabilize their presence
- Strong opposing beliefs may resist their manipulation

These weaknesses are not universal, but they represent common methods by which mortals attempt to counter Fey influence as it is base of the Fey's mentality, and personal magical strength. Although according to some scholars these weakeness are counter of their abilities and power.
#### **Connection & Ties :**
Fey have many connections and ties to different types of magic and fundamental forces of the universe that most mortals cannot fully comprehend. However, Fey are able to grasp and even master these concepts due to their unique way of thinking. Unlike mortals, whose understanding is often broad and structured, Fey perception is driven by emotion and focused on their own abstract ideals.
##### **Imaginatio & Names / Fey Pact :**
The Fey are considered one of the closest natural users of [[Imaginatio]]. Unlike mortals, who must learn or struggle to believe in their own imagination, most Fey exist in a state where imagination and reality are already intertwined.

Because of this, many Fey do not distinguish between what is real and what is imagined. Their thoughts, emotions, and stories can directly influence the world around them. In areas where Fey presence is strong, reality itself may begin to behave in inconsistent or symbolic ways.

This power is the reason why names are sacred in the eyes of the Fey. The sharing of a True Name, as well as the formation of agreements or bargains, creates a powerful bond. Such bonds are enforced through the pathway of [[Imaginatio]], binding individuals in ways that can be devastating if broken, depending on the power of the Fey involved. However, Fey rarely break their own agreements, as their connection to [[Imaginatio]] enforces these rules upon them with great strength.

Some scholars believe that the [[Feywild]] itself is partially shaped by [[Imaginatio]], making it a realm where imagination holds far greater authority over reality. However, very few reliable accounts from the [[Feywild]] have been recorded.
##### **Nature :**
Fey are deeply tied to natural environments, though not in a purely physical sense. Instead, they embody the _concept_ or _emotion_ of nature rather than its biological form. This connection allows them to access forms of power similar to [[Druidic Magic]] within their surroundings.

A forest inhabited by Fey may grow based on emotion rather than seasons. Rivers may change course based on stories or memories tied to them. As such, Fey influence often causes nature to behave in unnatural yet symbolic ways.

This connection makes Fey both protectors and distorters of nature, depending on their individual nature and intent.
