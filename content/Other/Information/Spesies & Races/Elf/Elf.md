**Elves** are a category of species most easily recognized by their long ears. They possess many different sub-species, a result of their unique ability to adapt to the environments around them. Over generations, Elven populations gradually absorb traits from their surroundings, allowing them to thrive in a wide variety of climates and regions throughout the [[Multiverse]].

Their long history and interactions with other [[Races & Species]] have often been marked by conflict. As a [[Long Life Species]], Elves can live long enough to remember events that other races consider ancient history. Combined with their strong cultural ties and interconnected communities, this has made it difficult for old grudges, alliances, and historical disputes to be forgotten.

### Origin :
The origins of the Elves can be traced back to [[Aranyani]]. After a fierce battle against [[Viradha]], one of the powerful [[Imaginatio Aberration]]s created by [[Barkakit]], both combatants were left gravely wounded. Upon returning to the [[Feywild]], [[Aranyani]]'s greenish-yellow blood spilled onto the soil. Several days later, the first Elves emerged from the land itself.

These first Elves were born with a natural affinity for [[Druidic Magic]] and quickly established their own societies within the [[Feywild]]. Over time, they formed various factions among the [[Fey Courts]]. These original Elves would later become known as the [[Eladrin]], often regarded as the purest descendants of the first Elven bloodline.

As Elven civilization expanded, many traveled through portals to the [[Plane of Terra]]. There, they gradually adapted to the environments in which they settled. This gave rise to numerous Elven sub-species, such as the [[Sea Elf]]s of the oceans and the [[Wood Elf]]s of the forests. Others embraced the traditions of scholarship and magical study found among the peoples of Terra, combining their druidic heritage with [[Arcane Magical Energy]] to become the [[High Elf]]s.
#### The Traitor Lolth :
Not all Elves share the same origin. The [[Drow]] are believed to have been created by [[Lolth]] after she gained access to the [[Dagger of Traitor]], the blessed weapon of [[Limia]]'s [[Vicariant]]. 

According to legend, Lolth used the dagger to slay [[Aranyani]] before fleeing to the [[Plane of Terra]] (To be exact the [[Underdark]]). There, she used the spilled blood of Aranyani to create the first [[Drow]], giving rise to a separate Elven lineage distinct from all others.

### Conflict :
Due to the Elves having a lot of connection with other [[Races & Species]], it cause forward many major conflict as it started even before the elves were born.
#### Majority of Elves towards Lolth & Drows :
The majority of elven [[Cultures]] are quite hostile toward and distrustful of the [[Drow]] due to [[Lolth]], the creator of the [[Drow]] and their [[Demi-Gods]], who slew [[Aranyani]], the creator of the other elves and their [[Demi-Gods]], with the [[Dagger of Traitor]].

Using [[Aranyani]]'s blood, [[Lolth]] created her own people, the [[Drow]], whom she ruled over within the [[Underdark]] beneath the [[Continent of Vallarian]]. This act caused the darkening of the lands within [[Inkwell Land]], where it is believed that [[Aranyani]]'s corpse and blood were brought to the [[Plane of Terra]] and used to create the drow.

Because of this act of aggression by [[Lolth]], the [[Drow]] are hated and seen as the enemies of all elves, as they are believed to have slain [[Aranyani]]. Trapped underground and subjected to [[Lolth]]'s talent for manipulation, the [[Drow of Underdark Culture Group]] developed into a hateful and somewhat tribal society where infighting is common, making them easier for [[Lolth]] to control.

This makes peace between the [[Drow]] and other elves almost impossible. The [[Drow]] already struggle to trust one another, so why would they trust the people who wish to kill them? Although in more human societies, [[Drow]] who do not follow the [[Drow of Underdark Culture Group]] created by [[Lolth]] are generally allowed to stay, their lives are often less peaceful than those of other [[Races & Species]]. The grudge that elves hold against them is well known among most [[Humanoid]] societies, and for those wishing to maintain good relations with elves, sacrificing the interests of the [[Drow]], who hold little influence in most societies. Is often seen as an easy price to pay.

#### Majority of Elves towards Orcs :
The majority of elves harbor a hatred toward the [[Orcs]], a race created from the blood of [[Viradha]], the greatest enemy of [[Aranyani]]. As a peaceful [[Imaginatio Aberration]], [[Aranyani]] wished to bring an end to violence, while [[Viradha]], despite also being an [[Imaginatio Author]], sought to spread violence and cultivate a warrior spirit throughout the world.

[[Aranyani]] ultimately succeeded in stopping [[Viradha]] by striking him with a spear crafted from the [[Banyan Root]]. She then used her [[Druidic Magic]] to cause the roots of the spear to spread throughout [[Viradha]]'s body, binding him within the [[Second Banyan Tree]].

Because of the conflict between their [[Demi-Gods]], much like the relationship between the elves and the [[Drow]], many elves developed a deep hatred toward the [[Orcs]]. They often view them as reflections of [[Viradha]] himself: power-hungry brutes who seek to take what they desire through force. This perception is reinforced by the traditions followed by many within the [[Orc Culture Group]], who continue to uphold teachings descended from [[Viradha]]. However, this belief often serves only as justification for elven prejudice against orcs, including those who have long since abandoned such [[Cultures]] and traditions.
