**Primordial Gods** are a term used for gods and goddesses that are created from [[Primordial Magical Energy]] and are believed to be able to tap into such unknown power, which is rarely accessible to any other deity. They control the foundational systems of the Multiverse itself, allowing them to possess broad power in a sense.

These gods are believed to exist to protect the balance of the universe. While some possess the power of their [[Divine Epithet]] fully within their domain, others only have partial control. One example is [[Talia the Weaver]], who can influence the [[River of Time]] but is not time itself.

### Primordials Gods List :
The amount of true primordials gods that exist is quite small :

- [[Lord Death]]
- [[Six Stars of Fate]]
#### Sudo-Primordial Gods :
The term for beings that possess or control [[Primordial Magical Energy]] is quite broad. However, there is a specific term for extremely powerful entities that exist within the multiverse and can influence or control this [[Magical Energy]]: [[Sudo-Primordial Gods]].

These beings are not as powerful as true Primordial Gods, but they are still capable of influencing the most fundamental and potent form of magic. This allows them to manipulate [[Primordial Magical Energy]], which can result in shifts in fate or the destiny of the universe itself similar.
#### Origin of the Term :
While the title _Primordial Gods_ was not only given by mortals, records indicate that it has been in use since [[Before Chronicles (BC)]]. Other [[Demi-Gods]], such as the [[Guardians of Titanfall]], also refer to them by this title. Even though the [[Guardians of Titanfall]] are created from the beliefs of their people and the core of the [[Plane of Terra|Material Plane]], they are not considered equal, as their domain is limited to [[Titanfall Isle]] and they cannot fully manipulate it.

One document recounts a mortal speaking to [[Naamah, The Guardian of Light]]:

> "These 'Primordial Gods' are simply able to control the functions of the universe itself, my friend. Their unbound power is beyond even the Guardians themselves."

It then adds:

> "Although it seems we cannot rely on them fully. Their power is unbound, but if the universe were to fall because of their actions, it would only bring even greater danger. So, as mortals you are, hope still lies in your hands. We shall help you fight, oh dear mortal friend."
