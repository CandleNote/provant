The Demi-Plane Theory is one of many theories created by the [[First Herrschers]] during his travels across the [[Continent of Vallarian]] with [[Kagri]]. He formed this theory after observing strange events where [[Demi-Planes]] appeared to form naturally, without mortal involvement.

Through his research, he concluded that [[Demi-Planes]] are created by **massive concentrations of [[Magical Energy]]**, not by a creature using their magic. This energy usually comes from powerful magical artifacts or unstable, uncontrolled [[Arcane Magical Energy]] or even [[Primordial Magical Energy]].

He determined that a Demi-Plane requires a **fixed point in space** saturated with magic equal to **[[8th Circle Magic]]**, a level of power far beyond what mortals, and even all [[Demi-Gods]] or [[Primordial Gods]] such as [[Talia the Weaver]], can control or access.

However, raw magic alone is not enough.
The [[First Herrschers]] theorized that magic must also take **shape**, much like a seed forming into a world. A Demi-Plane requires a guiding concept that defines its form and nature. He identified this force as **[[Memoria]]**, the will or memory carried by magic itself. While he initially doubted that magic could possess intent, later research by the [[1st Herrschers of Memory]] confirmed that magic can develop purpose when influenced by [[Memoria]].

From this, he concluded that **all [[Demi-Planes]] require two elements**:
- A vast amount of [[Magical Energy]]
- [[Memoria]], which gives form, meaning, and direction

Because [[Memoria]] provides structure and intent, it is often the true reason [[Demi-Planes]] come into existence. Most [[Demi-Planes]] therefore resemble fragments or reflections of reality rather than entirely new worlds.
According to the [[Intelligentsia Guild]], [[Demi-Planes]] are also **temporary constructs**, lasting only as long as their [[Magical Energy]] remains stable. This idea was later expanded upon by the [[2nd Herrschers of Memory]] in the **[[2nd Demi-Plane Theory]]**.
