## 1. Work Together In Character or Out of Character
You don’t need to agree on everything in character, but try to keep the story moving and avoid disrupting other players. If most of the group wants to do something, try not to break away or cause problems, since that makes the game less fun for everyone.
#### 1.1 Create a Character Who Can Work With the Group
Create a character who can travel with others and cooperate. Avoid characters who constantly argue, wander off, or refuse to help, because they slow down the game. Even if your character has a strong personality, they should still be able to work with the party. This is explain further in [[How to Make Player Character]]
## 2. Unable to make it for a session
If you can’t make it to a session, please tell your [[Dungeon Master]] as soon as you can, even if it’s only a few hours before. It’s always better to inform them than to say nothing at all.
#### 2.1 Send notice to Direct Message 
Please send the message through **their Direct Message** instead of the group chat. Telling everyone at once can lead to [[Social Contagion]], which could cancel the entire session entirely.
## 3. Let Everyone Have a Turn/Equal Screen Time
Give each player space to act, roleplay, and participate. Avoid hogging the spotlight. As you are not **The** Main Character of the game, but one of main characters within the game. 
## 4. Avoid Metagaming In Character
Don’t let player knowledge affect character decisions. Act on what your character knows, not what you know outside the game.
## 5. Respect the Table and Each Other
Keep the table welcoming. Don’t interrupt others, don’t mock ideas, and avoid making anyone uncomfortable. Everyone is here to have fun, so treat each other with basic respect.
## 6. Keep Side Conversations Low
Talking is fine, but try not to talk over the game. Too many side conversations make it hard for others to focus and can slow everything down.

Side conversations are like talking about matters that doesn't relate to the game for too long. Suggested to make these jokes in chat base instead of the voice call.
## 7. Respect the DM's Rulings
Sometimes the rules aren’t perfectly clear. If your [[Dungeon Master]] makes a ruling, please go with it for the moment. You can talk about it after the session if needed, but arguing in the moment slows things down.
## 8. Keep Phones and Distractions Low
It’s alright to check your phone, but try not to let it pull you out of the game too often. Staying focused helps everyone stay engaged and keeps the story moving.
