Social contagion is when one person’s choice or excuse quietly spreads through a group until everyone starts copying it. It makes people stop thinking for themselves and follow whatever is easiest. If one person backs out, suddenly everyone else starts doing the same, not because they actually need to, but because it feels safer to hide behind the first person’s decision.

It turns a group into a line of dominoes. One falls, and the rest lean with it. Instead of acting on their own judgment, people slip into imitation, letting someone else’s hesitation become their own.
