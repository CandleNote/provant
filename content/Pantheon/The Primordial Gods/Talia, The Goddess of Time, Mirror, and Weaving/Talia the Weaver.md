**Talia** (**TAL-ee-ah**), the **Goddess of Time, Mirrors, and Weaving**, is the divine guardian of the [[River of Time]] across the multiverse. She is one of only two known [[Primordial Gods]], the other being [[Lord Death]]. The origin of her godhood is unknown, though most scholars believe she has existed since the [[Start of the Universe]] itself.

Talia’s authority extends beyond merely protecting the flow of time. She also governs [[Memoria]], the memory and will bound to magic, and disciplines closely tied to time, such as divination. Though the [[River of Time]] is hidden behind the [[Shroud of Time]], [[Divination Magic]] allows mortals to glimpse _possible_ futures. These visions are never certain, as certainty would threaten the stability of time itself.
#### Symbols :
Talia’s holy symbol is a **mirror**, often worn as a pendant or incorporated into tools and attire. Mirrors represent reflection, possibility, and fractured truth. 

A **cracked mirror** is reserved for her most devout followers and [[Wayfarer]]. It symbolizes the many refractions of fate and the acceptance that no single future is whole or absolute. Talia's [[Wayfarer]] also use Mirror Orchid as a symbol for their goddess, as this beautiful flower have a reflective like inside that remind people of a mirror.
#### Origin :
Talia’s origin is quite unknown. Her existence begin since the start of the universe itself. No one knows if she was a mortal like every other gods before their own accession or just born to godhood. The only thing is for certain that she, and the River of Time always exist since the [[Start of the Universe]].
#### Tenets of the Time :
Followers of Talia are expected to uphold these principles:
- **Time must flow** — it may be observed, but never controlled.
- **Certainty is a lie** — the future is possibility that is the hand of everyone, not destiny.
- **Reflection precedes action** — to act without understanding consequence is to invite fracture.
#### Power :
Talia holds dominion over [[Chronomancy]], but its practice is strictly restricted by her Order. Rather than allowing time manipulation to spread, Talia and her followers work to **keep Chronomancy out of mortal hands**, treating it as a dangerous force that can fracture the [[River of Time]].

Chronomancy is not used to rewrite events, but to **counter, suppress, or correct** those who attempt to abuse time itself. Any direct manipulation of time is considered a severe threat to reality, and those who act without restraint risk drawing Talia’s judgment.

Talia is also known to be the only Goddess within the universe that is not under the rule of [[Pact of Lord Death]], which is believe due to her being [[Primordial Gods]], and have the trust of [[Lord Death]]

#### Nickname :
Like many gods, Talia is known by several names given by mortals and her [[Wayfarer]].
- **The Silent Goddess** : her most common title, earned through her near-total nonintervention. Even before the [[Pact of Lord Death]], Talia was known to observe rather than act.
- **The Master Diviner** : a title reflecting her authority over [[Divination Magic]]. Those who peer too deeply or too often into the future risk her attention, as excessive foresight can destabilize the flow of time itself.
#### Holy Laws & Taboo
**<u>Holy Laws</u>**
- **Time must flow** — it may be observed, never controlled.
- **Chronomancy is restricted** — used only to stop abuse of time.
- **The future is not certain** — foresight shows possibility, not fate.
- **Think before acting** — careless actions fracture time.
- **Stabilize, don’t rewrite** — correct disturbances without changing history.
- **Memory is sacred** — [[Memoria]] must not be altered or erased.

<u>Taboos</u>
- **Rewriting the past** is forbidden.
- **Claiming certainty** about the future is a lie.
- **Weaponized Chronomancy** is heresy.
- **False prophecies** corrupt the River.
- **Using time to escape judgment** is forbidden.
- **Replaying events for personal gain** corrupts [[Memoria]] of the world.
- **Forcing fate upon others** violates the humanity that Talia possess.
