**Vethya** (Vet-thi-ya), the Goddess of Winter, Hunting, and Family, is one of the youngest [[Gods & Goddess]] in the [[Continent of Vallarian]]. Even so, she commands a vast following, as her [[Divine Epithet|Divine Domains]] are widely embraced by the [[Common Folks]] of [[Continent of Vallarian|Valla]], more so than those of nearly any other deity, with the notable exception of [[Tryarha Mithso]] as [[Realm of Celestia]] is unbeatable on the popularity scaling.

Both Vethya and her [[Wayfarer]] teach that family is not something one is merely born into, but something one chooses. To them, true family consists of those who stand beside you through hardship, share in your victories, and hunt alongside you until the end of your journey. As to keep yourself warm from the cold winter, one must be surrounded by a welcoming fire instead of a hot burning melting flame.

#### Symbols :
The primary symbol of Vethya is an **owl**, representing family, loyalty, and companionship. According to her ascension stories, her familiar **Eirwyn** was an owl who later became the [[Huntress's Owl]].

Another common symbol is **Erica carnea** (Winter Heather), a flower that blooms during winter and can be found throughout [[Everwinter]] where there are barely any other vegetation

#### Nickname
Like many gods, Vethya is known by several names given by mortals and their [[Wayfarer]].
- **The Winter Huntress** : her most common title, reflecting her dominion over winter and hunting. It is especially popular among hunters and those who live within the harsh wilderness of [[Everwinter]]
- **The Sister of the Hunt** : Her more uncommon nickname is usually reserved for hunters and those who closely follow the hunting aspect of her [[Divine Epithet]]. As Vethya herself was a hunter when she was a [[Mortals]], many of her followers see her as family.

#### Origin :
Vethya was once a [[Half Elf]] who lived within what is now known as [[Everwinter Mountain]] that was born at 9050 AV. After the death of her biological parents during a harsh winter storm, she was adopted by the [[Talvi Family]], a human family of hunters. Growing up in the wilderness, she became a skilled huntress and learned [[Druidic Magic]], a talent common among the people of modern-day [[Everwinter]]. Through this magic, she summoned her owl companion, **Eirwyn**.

One day, Vethya's younger sister mysteriously disappeared. While many believed she had fallen victim to the "Monster of the Woods" and abandoned the search, Vethya refused to give up. Taking her bow and arrows, she ventured into the mountains in search of her sister. The creature was later believed to be a group of [[Tainted Creature]] led by a [[Fragments of the Eldar]], remnants of the ancient [[Eldar]] left behind after their invasion.

Vethya eventually found her sister mortally wounded. In her final moments, her sister fused with Eirwyn, becoming the [[Huntress's Owl]] and granting Vethya the strength needed to confront the creatures. What happened afterward remains unknown, but when the story ends, the young [[Half Elf]] had ascended to become one of the deities of the [[Druidic Pantheon]].

#### Power :
Vethya possesses the power of [[Druidic Magic]] like the other members of the [[Druidic Pantheon]], but she distinguishes herself through her focus on hunting and ranged combat. This manifests in the divine arrows that some of her [[Vicariant]] can wield, known as the [[Frostfang Arrow]], which can pierce any armor and freeze their enemies from within.

#### Relation :
Vethya played a major role in the creation of the [[Druidic Pantheon]] within [[Continent of Vallarian|Valla]]. Following her ascension, the rapid growth of her faith over just a few years attracted the attention of [[Lysithea]], who became concerned that Vethya's influence, particularly her unusually close relationship with the Lawful Evil deity [[Yilltasre]], could eventually lead to conflict among the various druidic faiths.

Fortunately, Vethya proved to be a peaceful and cooperative goddess. Many tales describe her as kind, approachable, and capable of mediating even with deities that are otherwise difficult to work alongside. Her ability to maintain positive relations with both allies and rivals helped ease tensions between the druidic gods.

As a result, the [[Pact of Season]] was formed, eventually becoming the foundation of the modern [[Druidic Pantheon]]. Through this agreement, Vethya was granted [[Everwinter Mountain]] as her [[Everseason Spots]], as it was the region where her [[Wayfarer]] were most heavily concentrated and where her influence was strongest.

#### Tenets of Winter & Hunt :
[[Wayfarer]] of Vethya are expected to uphold these principles:
- **Family is chosen** — bonds forged through trust and hardship are as strong as those of blood.
- **No hunter survives alone** — rely upon your companions as they rely upon you.
- **The winter rewards perseverance** — endure hardship with courage and determination.
- **Take only what is needed** — the hunt is for survival, not cruelty or excess.
- **Guide those lost in the cold** — offer warmth, shelter, and companionship to those in need.

#### Holy Laws & Taboo
**Holy Laws**
- **Protect your family** — whether by blood or choice, those under your care must never be abandoned.
- **Honor the hunt** — respect both predator and prey, and never kill without purpose.
- **Share warmth with others** — food, shelter, and companionship should be given to those enduring hardship.
- **Face adversity with resolve** — hardship is inevitable, but surrender is a choice.
- **Stand beside your companions** — loyalty is proven through action, not words.

**Taboos**
- **Abandoning family in their time of need** is a grave sin.
- **Hunting for sport or needless cruelty** is forbidden.
- **Betraying trusted companions** is condemned.
- **Taking more than one needs from the land** dishonors the hunt.
- **Denying aid to those who would perish from cold, hunger, or isolation** is considered an affront to Vethya.
