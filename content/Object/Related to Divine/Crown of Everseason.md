The Crown of Everseason is a relic created from the remnants of the [[Pact of Season]], a pact formed between the [[Druidic Pantheon]] to ensure peace among the [[Gods & Goddess]]. Although the crown was originally born from wishes of peace, old legends claim that it was actually forged by a [[Wayfarer]] of [[Lysithea]] who sought to bring everlasting joy to everyone.

Believing that a peaceful land without war or conflict could only exist under a strong ruler, this [[Wayfarer]] searched for the lingering [[Druidic Magic]] left within the [[Druidic Grove]] where the [[Pact of Season]] had first been agreed upon. There, the [[Wayfarer]] forged the Crown of Everseason in hopes of becoming powerful enough to protect their city, defend its people from outsiders, and rule as a mighty guardian.

However, the creation of the crown alarmed the [[Druidic Pantheon]]. Fearing that such power would eventually lead to disaster, the gods gathered a group of heroes to stop the [[Wayfarer]]. The heroes eventually succeeded, slaying the creator and shattering the Crown of Everseason into several pieces.

#### The Power of the Crown of Everseason
The Crown of Everseason possesses the power to control the surrounding nature, though its usage can also damage nature depending on the intentions and actions of its wielder. The crown is also capable of amplifying the existing abilities of the one who wears it.

After the heroes defeated its creator, the crown was broken into several fragments. Among these fragments, the gems were considered the most powerful pieces, and each was entrusted to one of the four heroes for safekeeping.

#### The Pieces of Crown of Everseason
The four heroes split the [[Crown of Everseason]] into several pieces after defeating the [[Wayfarer]] of [[Lysithea]]. The golden structure of the crown itself was broken into multiple fragments and entrusted to loyal allies of the heroes. Meanwhile, the crown’s central gemstone was divided into four separate pieces, each kept by one of the heroes.

As history continued, many [[Adventurers]], [[Cult]]s, and other individuals who obtained fragments of the relic were recorded using their power. Some legends even claim that one of the [[Six Stars of Fate]] wielded a fragment during the battle against the [[Enemy of Creation]]. Although many pieces have disappeared over time, others are believed to have been secretly collected and hidden away by nobles and influential families.
