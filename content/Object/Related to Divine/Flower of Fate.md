The **Flower of Fate** is a rare six-petaled lily closely associated with the [[Six Stars of Fate]]. Each of its six petals bears one of the colors of the [[Six Colour Stars]] : red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and violet. Due to this resemblance, many [[Wayfarer]] consider the flower a sacred symbol of hope, ambition, and defiance against fate.
## Overview
The Flower of Fate is among the rarest known plants in the Multiverse. First recorded during the [[Calamity Era]], it has never been successfully cultivated despite centuries of attempts by botanists, druids, alchemists, and mages. Every known specimen has appeared naturally, often in places connected to great acts of sacrifice, determination, or seemingly impossible victories.

Because of this, the flower is widely regarded as a blessing or omen rather than an ordinary plant.

## Connection to the Six Stars
The exact relationship between the Flower of Fate and the [[Six Stars of Fate]] remains unknown. However, records from throughout history suggest a strong correlation between sightings of the flower and the appearance of the [[Six Colour Stars]] within the [[Astral Sea]].

Many [[Wayfarer]] believe the flowers bloom when the will of the Six Stars reaches the mortal world. Others believe the flowers are remnants of the heroes themselves, left behind after the [[Calamity Event]]. Neither theory has been proven.

## Characteristics
Unlike ordinary lilies, the Flower of Fate possesses six petals arranged in a near-perfect star-like formation. Each petal displays one of six distinct colors corresponding to the [[Six Colour Stars]].

The flower does not produce seeds that can be cultivated, nor can it be reproduced through magical means. Attempts to transplant living specimens almost always fail, causing the flower to wither within days.

Because of this, most scholars classify the Flower of Fate as a supernatural phenomenon rather than a natural species.

## Religious Significance
Among the [[Wayfarer]], the Flower of Fate symbolizes the belief that destiny is never fixed. It serves as a reminder that even in the darkest circumstances, an individual possesses the ability to challenge fate and forge a new future.

Many shrines dedicated to the [[Six Stars of Fate]] display artistic depictions of the flower alongside the six-colored star emblem, making it one of the most recognizable symbols of the faith.
