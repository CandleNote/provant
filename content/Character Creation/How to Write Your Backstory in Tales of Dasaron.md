1. **Keep it short.**  
Your character’s story should not be very long.  
Try to stay under **3,000–4,000 characters**. (This is at most, and for NPC make it 2,000 to 3,000 characters)
If it feels too long, you probably added things that are not needed.

2. **Start with a simple list (the skeleton).**  
Write the important parts first, like :
- Where your character comes from
- Important events in their life
- Important people they met
- What happened to those people
- How these events changed your character
- How & Why they are able to do beyond normal humanoid capability 
It helps to write this in order from start to finish.

3. **Turn the list into a short story.**  
Use your list to write a simple backstory.  You don’t need fancy writing. Just explain what happened.  Your [[Dungeon Master]] is the one is reading this, so don’t hide anything.  
Show:
- Your character’s personality
- Their goal or motivation
- Why they act the way they do (May it be because of past occurrence or other factors (Inform [[Dungeon Master]] the reason as well))

4. **Keep it clear and simple.**  
It’s easy to write too much because it’s your character.  
But focus only on what matters:
- Important past events
- Personality
- Motivation
- Beliefs
If it helps the DM understand your character, keep it.  
If not, remove it.
