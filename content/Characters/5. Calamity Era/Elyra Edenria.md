# Chapter I : Early Life & Takao

In the quiet valley of Takao, in the country of Delstanvia, stood a small cloth shop owned by Astaria Edenria and Liebhaberin Edenria, travelers from the distant land of [[Mondral]]. Their daughter, Elyra Edenria, grew up among looms, dyed threads, and soft laughter.

From her parents, Elyra learned not only how to weave cloth, but how to wait. Her mother often said, “A good cloth isn’t made by force. # Chapter II : The Pathstrider of Talia

Above their hearth rested a small shrine: a silver mirror framed in brass, dedicated to Talia, the Weaver of past, present, and future. Elyra prayed there often, questioning whether the [[Talia the Weaver|Silent Goddess]] truly listened.

At times, when the room was still, Elyra thought her reflection moved a moment too late, or smiled before she did. At first it frightened her. Over time, it became comforting. She did not know why, only that it made her feel safe.
# Chapter III : The Plagues Which Pull Her Threads

Peace faded when travelers from the East spoke of a strange magical plague or some sort of spells failing, reflections acting on their own, and days lost to broken time. Most villagers dismissed the stories.

That night, as Elyra prayed, the candle flame bent sideways. In the mirror, her hands unraveled into light. She felt no fear, only a gentle pull, like a thread asking to be followed.
# Chapter IV : The Reason She Walks

Elyra left Takao at dawn with little more than a cloak, her prayer mirror, a spool of blue silk, and her parents’ blessing. “Please Stay Safe” her father told her.

Her father wished her safety, hiding his fear behind quiet strength. Elyra walked east, toward those seeking adventurers to face the plague others ignored, hoping to mend what was beginning to fray with the help others hopefully.
